Preparation

You will get the most out of this class if you (1) attend class, (2) do the homework, and (3) engage with the readings.

Problem sets

To practice coding in R you will complete problem sets throughout the semester. Feel free to work together on the problem sets but you must turn in your own answers. You cannot work in groups of more than three people.

  • ✔+: (22 out of 20) Assignment is 100% completed. Every question was attempted and answered correctly. Document is clean and easy to follow. I will not assign these often.
  • ✔: (20 out of 20) Assignment is mostly complete and most answers are correct. This is the expected level of performance.
  • ✔−: (10 out of 20) Assignment is mostly incomplete and/or most answers are incorrect. This indicates that you need to improve next time.

I’ll accept problem sets late one day (but you will lose a full letter-grade). Beyond that I won’t accept late problem sets.

Exams

There will be two exams covering (1) the first half of the course, especially programming, and (2) the second half of the course, especially causation.

Make-up exams will be given only to students with medical or personal emergencies. If an emergency arises, you will need to contact me before the exam or you will receive zero credit.
